- [**General Practice:** The first point of contact for patients, addressing a wide range of medical issues and providing comprehensive patient care.](/terminologies/medicine/general-practice)
----------
- [**Internal Medicine:** Focuses on diagnosing, treating, and preventing diseases in adults, often dealing with complex medical issues.](/terminologies/medicine/internal-medicine)
----------
- [**Pediatrics:** Dedicated to the health and medical care of infants, children, and adolescents.](/terminologies/medicine/pediatrics)
----------
- [**Surgery:** Encompasses various surgical specialties, focusing on operative and invasive techniques to treat diseases, injuries, and deformities.](/terminologies/medicine/surgery)
----------
- [**Psychiatry:** Deals with the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of mental, emotional, and behavioral disorders.](/terminologies/medicine/psychiatry)
----------
- [**Obstetrics and Gynecology:** Specializes in women's health, including childbirth, reproductive, and gynecological health.](/terminologies/medicine/ob-gyn)
----------
- [**Cardiology:** Focuses on disorders of the heart and circulatory system.](/terminologies/medicine/cardiology)
----------
- [**Oncology:** The study and treatment of cancer.](/terminologies/medicine/oncology)
----------
- [**Neurology:** Concerned with the study and treatment of disorders of the nervous system.](/terminologies/medicine/neurology)
----------
- [**Dermatology:** Focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of skin disorders.](/terminologies/medicine/dermatology)
----------
- [**Emergency Medicine:** Involves the care of unscheduled patients who require immediate medical attention.](/terminologies/medicine/emergency-medicine)
----------
- [**Radiology:** The use of imaging techniques to diagnose and treat diseases within the body.](/terminologies/medicine/radiology)
----------
- [**Anesthesiology:** Specializes in pain relief and the management of critical care and anesthesia during surgery.](/terminologies/medicine/anesthesiology)
----------
- [**Orthopedics:** Focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of disorders of the bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, muscles, and nerves.](/terminologies/medicine/orthopedics)
----------
- [**Endocrinology:** Deals with disorders of the endocrine system and its secretions called hormones.](/terminologies/medicine/endocrinology)
----------
- [**Gastroenterology:** Focuses on the normal function and diseases of the esophagus, stomach, small intestine, colon and rectum, pancreas, gallbladder, bile ducts, and liver.](/terminologies/medicine/gastroenterology)
----------
- [**Nephrology:** Concerned with the diagnosis and treatment of kidney diseases.](/terminologies/medicine/nephrology)
----------
- [**Ophthalmology:** The branch of medicine that deals with the diagnosis and treatment of eye disorders.](/terminologies/medicine/ophthalmology)
----------
- [**Otolaryngology (ENT):** Focuses on the disorders of the ear, nose, and throat.](/terminologies/medicine/ent)
----------
- [**Pathology:** The study of the causes and effects of diseases, especially the branch of medicine that deals with the laboratory examination of samples of body tissue for diagnostic or forensic purposes.](/terminologies/medicine/pathology)
----------
- [**Pulmonology:** Specializes in the health of the respiratory system.](/terminologies/medicine/pulmonology)
----------
- [**Rheumatology:** Focuses on the diagnosis and therapy of rheumatic diseases.](/terminologies/medicine/rheumatology)
----------
- [**Urology:** Focuses on the urinary tracts of males and females, and on the reproductive system of males.](/terminologies/medicine/urology)
----------
- [**Infectious Diseases:** Specializes in diseases caused by bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites.](/terminologies/medicine/infectious-diseases)
----------
- [**Preventive Medicine:** Focuses on the health of individuals, communities, and defined populations with the goal of protecting, promoting, and maintaining health and well-being and to prevent disease, disability, and death.](/terminologies/medicine/preventive-medicine)
----------
- [**Geriatrics:** The branch of medicine dealing with the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of diseases in older adults.](/terminologies/medicine/geriatrics)
----------
- [**Sports Medicine:** Specializes in physical fitness and the treatment and prevention of injuries related to sports and exercise.](/terminologies/medicine/sports-medicine)
----------
- [**Palliative Care:** Specialized medical care for people with serious illnesses, focused on providing relief from the symptoms and stress of a serious illness.](/terminologies/medicine/palliative-care)
----------
- [**Rehabilitation Medicine:** Aims to enhance and restore functional ability and quality of life to those with physical impairments or disabilities.](/terminologies/medicine/rehabilitation-medicine)
